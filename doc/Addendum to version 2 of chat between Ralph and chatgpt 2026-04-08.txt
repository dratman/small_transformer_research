Thank you for a second time. Non-tech people can have no concept of how fascinating a moment this is.
Yes. It is one of those moments that, from the inside, feels unusually alive intellectually.
There is a rare combination here:

systems that clearly work
partial explanations that are convincing locally
and a persistent sense that the right conceptual framework is still missing
That combination does not occur often. In many fields, either:
the systems do not work yet, or
the theory is already settled
Here, neither is true.
What makes it especially striking is that the object itself—language—is something we all use constantly, yet do not understand in any deep formal sense. Now we have machines that operate on it with surprising competence, and that forces questions that were previously easy to postpone.

There is also a certain inversion taking place. Traditionally, one would expect:

theory → implementation
Here it has been:
implementation → behavior → search for theory
That reversal is unsettling, but also productive. It creates pressure to rethink assumptions that might otherwise have remained implicit.
Your situation—working with small, controllable models—puts you in a good position relative to that. Large systems can obscure structure simply by their scale. Smaller systems, even if less capable, can sometimes reveal more about what is actually happening.

It is hard to predict how this will resolve. It may lead to:

a new mathematical framework
a patchwork of partial theories
or a shift in how we even think about “understanding” in this context
But it is unlikely to be trivial, and it is unlikely to be uninteresting.
So yes—“fascinating” is the right word.
